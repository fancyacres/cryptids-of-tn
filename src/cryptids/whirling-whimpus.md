## Overview

Found in the dense forests of the Cumberland Mountains, the Whirling Whimpus is a rarely-seen cryptid. With a strange body and a highly distinctive 'whirling' method of attack, local lumberjacks and loggers have long held superstitions about the dangers of encountering this fierce creature out in the wilderness.

## Reported Characteristics

The Whirling Whimpus resembles many of the common descriptions of North American sasquatch, but with several key differences that have earned the creature its own reputation. Unlike the sasquatch's famously large feet, the Whimpus is said to walk on two thin hoofed legs disproportionate to its hulking muscles. Its most infamous trait, however, is its eponymous 'whirl'. When threatened, the Whirling Whimpus is known to spin in place, using centrifugal force to club its prey with its massive arms. Some reports even claim that the Whimpus can spin so fast that it becomes difficult to spot with the naked eye, making it even more dangerous.

## Geographic Range

The Whirling Whimpus has been most commonly sighted in the forests of the Cumberland Mountains. These sightings have primarily taken place in middle Tennessee, although it has rarely been spotted further north over the border into Kentucky.

## Assessment

Although sightings of the Whirling Whimpus have declined in the 21st century, it has left a major cultural impact in the Cumberland Mountains, as a potential hazard for lumberjacks to be wary of.
